I am a fifth-year Biostatistics PhD candidate in the Graduate Group in Epidemiology and Biostatistics (GGEB) at the University of Pennsylvania, a member of the Penn Statistics in Imaging and Visualization Center (PennSIVE), and a National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellow.

My current research is primarily motivated by statistical issues in the analysis of complex multi-modal neuroimaging data. Under the mentorship of Dr. Russell (Taki) Shinohara and Dr. Kristin Linn, I’ve worked to develop a number of broadly applicable statistical methods, including hypothesis tests for multiple neuroimaging modalities, spatial localization of inter-modal associations, and feature extraction in the presence of nuisance variables.

I also completed my MS in Biostatistics at Penn under the mentorship of Dr. Alisa Stephens-Shields where we proposed leveraging observational data and propensity score modeling in the design of pragmatic clinical trials.

Before starting at Penn, I graduated from Columbia University in 2016 with a BA in Statistics and Psychology. From 2016-2018, I worked as a data analyst in anesthesia research at the Hospital for Special Surgery.
